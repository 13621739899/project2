RQ2: “What is the relationship, if any, between the monthly expenditure on media platform subscriptions and the age of individuals?”

library(tidyverse)

data <- read.csv("Project2.csv")

data$pay <- factor(data$pay, levels = c("$0 ", "$0-5", "$5-10", "$10-15", "$15-20", "$25+"), ordered = TRUE)

data$age <- factor(data$age, levels = sort(unique(data$age)), ordered = TRUE)

# Plot
ggplot(data, aes(x=pay, fill=age)) + 
  geom_bar() +
  ggtitle("the relationship between the monthly expenditure on media platform subscriptions and 
          different ranges of people(16-32)") +
  xlab("Monthly Expenditure on media platform subscriptions(dollar)") +
  ylab("number of people with range of ages") +
  labs(fill="Age") +
  scale_y_continuous(breaks = seq(0, max(table(data$pay)), by = 1)) + 
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(panel.grid.major = element_line(colour = "darkgray"), 
        panel.grid.minor = element_line(colour = "darkgray"))

From the diagram, we can see that younger people have a wide range of spending on media subscriptions, with a noticeable trend towards lower costs. On the other hand, older age groups seem to have a more stable spending behavior. However, the chart might miss some extreme cases or not represent all age groups, so looking into more detailed data might give a clearer picture.

data <- read.csv("Project2.csv")
library(dplyr)

results <- data %>%
  group_by(pay) %>%
  summarise(
    mean_age = mean(age),
    median_age = median(age)
  )

limitation: social desirability bias –> respondents’ desire to answer a question in a way they believe is morally or socially acceptable. in this survey, no one has really put down 1 in their ability to critically analyse the information presented

another limitation –> wording of the survey question –> very broad and can have two meanings associated with it.E.g. maybe scrutinizing the veracity of claims, or reading through it carefully/slowly, etc. Different respondents might approach the question differently
